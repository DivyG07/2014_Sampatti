Document Guidance

  2. Pages
  3. Document Guidance

  1. Agreement to Sell/Purchase:
    2. This document outlines the terms and conditions of the property transaction, including the sale price, payment schedule, and other relevant details. It should include provisions for remedies in case of breach of contract by either party.
  2. Sale Deed:
    3. The sale deed is the legal document that transfers ownership of the property from the seller to the buyer. It should be executed on a non-judicial stamp paper and registered with the local sub-registrar's office.
  3. Title Deed and Property Documents:
    4. Documents proving the legal ownership and title of the property, such as the original title deed, previous sale deeds, and property tax receipts, should be verified.
  4. Encumbrance Certificate:
    5. This certificate confirms that the property is free from any legal encumbrances or liabilities, such as mortgages, liens, or claims.
  5. No Objection Certificate (NOC):
    6. If applicable, NOCs may be required from relevant authorities, societies, or co-owners to confirm that there are no legal objections to the property transaction.
  6. Occupancy Certificate (OC) or Completion Certificate (CC):
    7. For newly constructed properties, an OC confirms that the property is ready for occupancy, while a CC indicates that the construction is complete as per approved plans.
  7. Power of Attorney (POA):
    8. If a person cannot be physically present for the transaction, they can authorize someone to act on their behalf using a power of attorney document.
  8. Loan Agreement and Mortgage Documents:
    9. If the buyer is availing a loan, the loan agreement and mortgage documents will outline the terms of the loan and the property's use as collateral.
  9. Agreement for Lease/Rental Agreement:
    10. In case of rental or lease transactions, an agreement specifying the terms, duration, rent, and other conditions should be executed.
  10. Diligence Reports:
    11. Property due diligence reports may include verification of ownership, title, encumbrances, and other legal aspects of the property.
  11. Possession Certificate:
    12. This certificate confirms the transfer of physical possession of the property to the buyer.
  12. Mutation Document:
    13. Mutation or transfer of property records the change of ownership with the local municipal authority.
  13. Society/Association Documents:
    14. For properties in housing societies or associations, documents related to membership, rules, and regulations should be reviewed.
  14. Tax Receipts and Clearance Certificates:
    15. Verify property tax payments and obtain clearance certificates from relevant authorities.
